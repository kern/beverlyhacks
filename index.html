FAQ

Are there ID requirements or an age limit to enter the event?

You must show your school ID. You must be a current high school student. You must bring your parental consent form.

What are my transport/parking options getting to the event?

We encourage for you to arrange transportation with your parents or carpool to the event.

What can/can't I bring to the event?

Bring your sleeping bags in case you want to nap during the event. We are following standard BHHS school code of conduct and will have strict security provided by the school. We have a Code of Conduct that each person must sign at registration.

As a hacker, what must I bring in order to enter the event?

You must bring (1) your valid school ID, (2) a printed copy of your signed parental consent form, and (3) your eventbrite ticket.

How big can my team be?

You can have a maximum of 4 people per team! We will be helping hackers find teammates during the hackathon.

Is my registration/ticket transferrable?

Your ticket is not transferrable. If you have an issue, please message Justin Brezhnev at brezh@hacker.fund immediately.

Where can I contact the organizer with any questions?

If you have any questions, feel free to message Justin Brezhnev at brezh@hacker.fund.

Are we allowed to build on past projects?

A hackathon is an experience to build something new within a set amount of time. Therefore, you cannot work on past projects. You may use your own open-source or third party libraries as long as the project is new.

Can parents attend the event?

We encourage the parents to attend the Opening/Expo/Closing Ceremonies of the hackathon. There is a ton of learning and creation happening during peak hours so if parents want to drop off items or communicate with their kids, feel free to contact Justin Brezhnev at (310) 963 9976 or Justin Ezor (310) 738 0683.

As a parent, what must I bring to enter?

You must bring (1) your valid photo ID and (2) your eventbrite ticket.

The name on the registration/ticket doesn't match the attendee. Is that okay?

No. The name on the registration ticket must match the ID of the attendee.
